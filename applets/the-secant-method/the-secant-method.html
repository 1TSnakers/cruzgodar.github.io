Nova Fractals

Like most fractals, Newton’s method can be generalized to produce incredible — and wildly chaotic — results. Changing the original formula \(x_{n + 1} = x_n - \frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)}\) to \(x_{n + 1} = x_n - a\frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)} + c\) gives us two new parameters to modify — here, \(a\) and \(c\) are represented by the red and blue dots, respectively. The applet typically gives interesting results when \(a\) is on the right half of the canvas and not too far from the middle vertically. The farther it is to the right, the further \(c\) can move away from the center while still producing good results.
