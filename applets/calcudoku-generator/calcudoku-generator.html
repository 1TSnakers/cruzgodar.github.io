Calcudoku Generator

Calcudoku is a Sudoku-like puzzle — given an \(n \times n\) grid, fill it withn the digits \(1, ..., n\) such that no row or column contains a repeated digit, and such that each cage’s condition is satisfied. For example, a cage with \(5+\) in a corner must have its contents sum to 5. A cage with \(2-\) is a little more complicated — it means that choosing some cell in the cage and subtracting the others results in 2. A 3-cell cage containing 5, 2, and 1 would work. \(:\) is the division operator, and works similarly to subtraction. Finally, cages with no operation at all are always \(1 \times 1\), and simply contain the number in the corner.


Generating a calcudoku puzzle is no easy feat, and this algorithm is far from the fastest, but it will generate a puzzle of any given size. These vary wildly in difficulty, but every one is guaranteed to have a unique solution. If the puzzles are consistently too difficult, try limiting the cage size to 2 or 3 or 4.
