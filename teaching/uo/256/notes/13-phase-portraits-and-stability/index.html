Our final topic will be nonlinear systems, like the Lotka-Volterra model for predator-prey relationships. Some of the methods we’ll use to understand and classify them apply to linear systems too, and so we’ll start there.

Let’s let $\mathbf{A}$ be a $2 \times 2$ matrix and look at the system $\mathbf{x}' = \mathbf{Ax}$. As we touched on in homework 8, the eigenvalues of $\mathbf{A}$ determine the general behavior of the system — let’s summarize the possibilities here. First of all, we’ll need a few definitions of vector field concepts

Definition: sources, sinks, and saddles

In a vector field, a critical point is a point $(x, y)$ for which there is no motion. If the field around a critical point exclusively flows away from it, the point is called a source. If the surrounding field exclusively flows toward the point, it’s called a sink. Otherwise, the point is called a saddle point.
