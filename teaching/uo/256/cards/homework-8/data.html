12. A remarkable property of the determinant is that it’s multiplicative: for any two $n \times n$ matrices $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$, $\det (\mathbf{AB}) = \left( \det \mathbf{A} \right)\left( \det \mathbf{B} \right)$. Use this property to answer the following questions.

a) What is $\det \mathbf{I}$? Hint: $\mathbf{IA} = \mathbf{A}$ for any matrix $\mathbf{A}$ for which the product makes sense.

b) Let $\mathbf{A}$ be an invertible matrix. What is $\det \left(\mathbf{A}^{-1}\right)$ in terms of $\det \mathbf{A}$?

13. Suppose $\mathbf{A}$ is an invertible matrix with eigenvectors $\mathbf{v_1}, ..., \mathbf{v_n}$ with corresponding eigenvalues $\lambda_1, ..., \lambda_n$.

a) For $\mathbf{A}$ to be invertible, none of the $\lambda_i$ can be zero. Why is this?

b) What are the eigenvectors and eigenvalues of $\mathbf{A}^{-1}$? Hint: start with $\mathbf{Av_i} = \lambda_i \mathbf{v_i}$ and find a way to introduce $\mathbf{A}^{-1}$ into the equation.
