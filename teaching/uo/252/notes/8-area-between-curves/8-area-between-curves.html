!header Section 8: The Area Between Curves !begin-text-block Up to this point, we’ve defined integrals via Riemann sums, related them to antiderivatives via the Fundamental Theorem, and then made taking antiderivatives a little easier with $u$-sub. Now, we’ll extend what definite integrals can do by exploring how they can calculate area not just under a curve, but between two of them. !end-text-block !begin-prop area between curves Let $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ be functions with $f(x) \geq g(x)$ when $a \leq x \leq b$. Then the area between the graphs of $f$ and $g$ on $[a, b]$ $$\int_a^b (f(x) - g(x))\ dx.$$ !end-prop !begin-text-buttons previous l Previous home l Home next l Next !end-text-buttons !footer
