!header Section 4: Comparison Tests
!begin-text-block Our next test for convergence isn’t nearly so complicated. Now that we know the behavior of a few series, like all of the $p$-series, we can also determine how other closely related series behave. One limit law for sequences states that if $a_n \geq b_n$ eventually (meaning for all $n \geq N$ for some integer $N$), then $\lim (a_n) \geq \lim (b_n)$, assuming both exist. Applying this to the sequence of partial sums for two series gives us the following straightforward result. !end-text-block !begin-prop the comparison test Let $\sum_{n = 1}^\infty a_n$ and $\sum_{n = 1}^\infty b_n$ be two series with $a_n \geq b_n \geq 0$ for all $n \geq N$ for some integer $N$. Then: 1. If $\sum_{n = 1}^\infty a_n$ converges, then $\sum_{n = 1}^\infty b_n$ also converges. 2. If $\sum_{n = 1}^\infty b_n$ diverges, then $\sum_{n = 1}^\infty b_n$ also diverges. !end-prop !begin-text-block The idea isn’t too complicated: if you start with a convergent series with all positive terms and decrease all of them, the new series will still converge.
