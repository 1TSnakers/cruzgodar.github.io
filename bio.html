Me
Beginnings
I was born in Healdsburg, Califnoria, and moved to San Luis Obispo after just a year. I was homeschooled, and took to math at an early age — my parents tell me I understood the concept of pi as it relates to circles when I was three. I started reading the same year, and that may be why I developed grapheme-color synesthesia, a curious, harmless condition that I consider myself fortunate to have.

I gathered a working knowledge in most subjects, but in math, I thrived, learning most of (high school) algebra by the time I was eleven. I started thinking about going further, and so it was that my parents and I began to learn about how an eleven-year-old could attend community college.
University Days
In the end, it was easier than we thought. Though it limited me to eleven units a semester, I was able to register as an enrichment student at Cuesta, our local community college. Being surrounded by students ten or forty years older than me was a daunting experience, but one I adapted to quickly. Over the next four years, I took twenty-one classes, worked as a tutor, and eventually applied and was accepted to Cal Poly, the local university. I was thrilled.

Cal Poly was far more challenging than Cuesta, but I made it through two years of classes and research, even finding a community in the university’s Math Lounge, which I eventually became the unofficial leader of. Toward the end of my first year, I began playing with the idea of writing a book, and I whittled an outline in my spare time over the following year. I graduated from Cal Poly in Spring 2018 with honors.
Forward
I chose to take a gap year before attending grad school to travel to Germany. There, I am attending a language school for a few months before traveling the country, WWOOFing. I’ll return to San Luis sometime in April, see many of my friends graduate in June, and then leave for grad school. It is an exciting, hectic, and somewhat terrifying time.
