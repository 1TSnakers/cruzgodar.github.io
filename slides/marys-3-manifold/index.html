What's a Geometry, Anyway?

A geometry on a manifold $X$ is a bilinear form $\left< \cdot, \cdot \right>$ defined on each tangent space $T_p X$ that is symmetric and nondegenerate, and varies smoothly across tangent spaces.

A Riemannian metric is one that's also positive definite (matching the usual definition of an inner product).
